# 5 Top Data Ideas

## 1. Waiting List Flow Simulator

**What it is:**  
A decision-support tool for Irish healthcare planners that models waiting list pressure and lets users test interventions like extra clinics, added capacity, or specialty-specific staffing.

**Why it’s strong:**
- very obvious public value
- easy to explain in a 5-minute pitch
- strong “AI helping humans make better decisions” framing
- good ethics story because it supports planners instead of replacing them

**Likely data:**
- NTPF waiting list data
- HSE waiting/performance data
- potentially deprivation overlays for equity analysis

**Demo shape:**
- current waiting list trends
- slider/toggles for interventions
- projected impact chart with uncertainty band

---

## 2. Flood Nowcast + Human Validation Loop

**What it is:**  
A short-horizon flood risk assistant combining OPW water levels and Met Éireann forecasts, with a human validation layer for local knowledge.

**Why it’s strong:**
- strong climate resilience story
- visually demoable with maps
- very aligned with “human intelligence driving innovation”
- can look polished quickly

**Likely data:**
- OPW real-time water levels
- Met Éireann forecast data
- flood zones and spatial layers

**Demo shape:**
- map with stations/catchments
- risk score over the next few hours
- explainers for rain, river levels, and thresholds
- human feedback loop

---

## 3. Planning Pipeline Forecaster

**What it is:**  
A tool for forecasting likely planning approvals and commencements and surfacing housing delivery bottlenecks by place and time.

**Why it’s strong:**
- housing is highly relevant and legible
- strong Irish public data angle
- practical mix of technical depth and public impact
- supports a clear narrative around infrastructure and housing delivery

**Likely data:**
- planning register datasets
- commencement/building control datasets
- property/valuation or geographic overlays

**Demo shape:**
- map/list of planning activity
- predicted approval/commencement trends
- risk-of-delay indicators
- explainability panel

---

## 4. Road Safety Hotspot Prioritiser

**What it is:**  
An explainable model highlighting dangerous road segments and helping prioritise interventions.

**Why it’s strong:**
- easy public-interest story
- map-based demo will land well
- solid technical story without needing frontier AI
- explainability is natural here

**Likely data:**
- RSA / CSO collision data
- TII traffic counts
- weather, road context, and geospatial layers

**Demo shape:**
- hotspot map
- ranked risks
- top contributing factors
- suggested intervention categories

---

## 5. Retrofit Prioritisation with Fairness Controls

**What it is:**  
A system that helps identify where retrofit investment could deliver the best mix of energy improvement and social equity.

**Why it’s strong:**
- climate + cost of living + fairness is a strong narrative
- excellent ethics/explainability angle
- likely to resonate as socially useful AI

**Likely data:**
- BER statistical outputs
- deprivation index
- Census/small area data
- housing stock/building age proxies

**Demo shape:**
- map of priority areas
- ranking by carbon benefit / equity benefit / blended score
- explainable scoring breakdown

---

## Recommended shortlist

Best three overall:
1. Waiting List Flow Simulator
2. Flood Nowcast + Human Validation Loop
3. Planning Pipeline Forecaster

If one needs to be picked quickly, **Waiting List Flow Simulator** is the best all-round balance of impact, feasibility, and pitch strength.
