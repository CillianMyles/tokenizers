# 5 Health Data Ideas

## 1. Waiting List Flow Simulator

**Best overall pick**

**What it is:**  
A planning tool that models Irish waiting list pressure and lets users test interventions like extra clinics, staffing boosts, or specialty prioritisation.

**Why it’s strong:**
- immediately understandable problem
- huge public relevance in Ireland
- easy to demo
- strong ethics framing: supports planners, does not replace them

**Likely data:**
- NTPF waiting list datasets
- HSE waiting/performance data

**Demo:**
- current wait trends
- “what if” controls
- projected outcomes with uncertainty and explanations

---

## 2. Care Access Equity Lens

**Best health + fairness idea**

**What it is:**  
A tool that combines waiting list/service access data with deprivation or geographic data to show where poor access and disadvantage overlap.

**Why it’s strong:**
- very aligned with public-service impact
- strong ethics and equity story
- more interesting than a plain dashboard

**Likely data:**
- waiting list/open health access data
- Census small-area data
- deprivation index

**Demo:**
- interactive map
- identify underserved areas
- rank areas by combined pressure and disadvantage

---

## 3. Service Navigation Copilot for Public Health Information

**Best user-facing product**

**What it is:**  
A grounded assistant that helps people navigate public health service information such as waiting times, service types, pathways, definitions, and next steps.

**Why it’s strong:**
- very demoable
- natural use of LLMs without diagnosis territory
- can be grounded entirely in official sources

**Likely data/content:**
- HSE public service pages
- waiting time pages
- official public-facing health guidance

**Demo:**
- ask plain-English questions
- assistant answers with citations to official content
- explains confusing service terms

---

## 4. Waiting List Risk / Bottleneck Detector

**Best analytics-heavy health build**

**What it is:**  
A system that spots specialties, hospitals, or regions where waiting list pressure is deteriorating faster than normal and flags likely bottlenecks.

**Why it’s strong:**
- narrower than the simulator, so easier to build
- feels intelligent without requiring a giant modelling stack
- good fit for anomaly detection / forecasting / explainability

**Likely data:**
- NTPF historical waiting list data
- HSE performance data

**Demo:**
- trend detection
- anomaly flags
- “what changed?” explanation panel

---

## 5. Child Protection Capacity Early Warning Dashboard

**Highest-impact but highest-care option**

**What it is:**  
A dashboard that uses Tusla performance metrics to identify capacity strain, rising backlogs, or emerging pressure points for managers.

**Why it’s strong:**
- serious real-world importance
- clearly human-in-the-loop
- strong operational/public-service story

**Likely data:**
- Tusla performance datasets
- optional geography overlays

**Demo:**
- service pressure trends
- flagged areas
- investigation prompts for managers

---

## Recommended shortlist

Top three:
1. Waiting List Flow Simulator
2. Care Access Equity Lens
3. Service Navigation Copilot

If one needs to win the room quickly, **Waiting List Flow Simulator** is the strongest choice.
